While Gen Z and Millenials have been at the center of the vaping conversation, older smokers are also turning to e-cigarettes, a mechanically similar habit, for a change as well.

In the U.S., nearly 60 percent of current adult e-cigarette smokers also smoke cigarettes, while 29 percent are former cigarette smokers. Even more … nearly 99 percent of e-cigarette smokers over the age of 45 are current or former smokers according to the latest Center for Disease Control (CDC) National Health Review Survey. What does this mean?

This means that Generation X and Xennials are just as important to Vape Away’s mission to cut harsh vaping chemicals for everyone.

It only makes sense that the generations who grew accustomed to smoking on airplanes and trains, in offices and restaurants. Vaping offers the same frequent, casual, and fairly public option of puffing wherever you’d like.

Before the pandemic took the world by storm in March 2020, the major stories in the daily news cycle revolved around respiratory hospitalizations and deaths due to vaping. These concerns have not faltered over the past year, making a solution to the habit’s harms even more crucial.

As researchers continued learning about the long-term effects of vaping, the unsupportive false narrative that e-cigarettes are a clean and environmentally sustainable alternative to cigarettes began to circulate. A new study from the University of Queensland in Australia is questioning the validity of this theory.

Addictive Behaviors, the Australian medical journal, published a study that revealed e-cigarettes may be more effective in helping smokers quit than what the study refers to as “nicotine replacement therapy”, including products like nicotine patches and gum. The trial assessed nearly 13 thousand participants in total across 16 different smoking and vaping trials.

When compared to the traditional tobacco cigarette, e-cigarettes proved to be 50% more effective than nicotine replacement therapy and 100% more effective than the placebo. For transitioning cigarette smokers, vaping offers the most comparable experience to the real thing. Bottom line: smokers who vape are more likely to quit smoking than smokers who don’t vape.
